# Section 3: Hackers - Who Are They?

## 14. Hacker Methodology (Summary)

---

## Overview

Hacking does not follow a strict playbook, but most cyber attacks follow a flexible process with common steps. These steps help attackers gather information, exploit weaknesses, and avoid detection.

---

## 1. Reconnaissance (Footprinting)

Hackers gather as much information as possible about a target.

### Types:
- **Passive Reconnaissance** (legal)
  - Collect publicly available information  
  - Examples: emails, phone numbers, social media, locations  

- **Active Reconnaissance** (illegal)
  - Actively probe systems for weaknesses  
  - Examples: IP addresses, DNS, open ports, usernames, software  

---

## 2. Exploitation

Attackers use discovered vulnerabilities to gain access.

### Common methods:
- Phishing emails  
- Social engineering  
- Weak passwords  
- Unpatched software  
- Malware injection  

---

## 3. Privilege Escalation

After gaining access, attackers increase their control.

### Actions:
- Create new accounts  
- Hijack systems or networks  
- Gain admin-level access  

---

## 4. Establishing Persistence

Attackers ensure they can maintain access even if discovered.

### Techniques:
- Backdoors  
- Remote access tools  

---

## 5. Attack Phase

Attackers execute their main objectives.

### Examples:
- Data theft (extraction)  
- Data corruption  
- Malware deployment  

---

## 6. Covering Tracks

Attackers attempt to avoid detection.

### Methods:
- Clearing logs  
- Erasing command history  
- Using covert channels (e.g., ICMP tunnels)  

---

## Key Insight

The more information attackers have about a target, the higher their chances of success.

---

## Recap

1. Reconnaissance  
2. Exploitation  
3. Privilege Escalation  
4. Persistence  
5. Attack  
6. Cover Up
