### Passive Reconnaissance

## 2. Exploitation Phase

Next will now be the Exploitation Phase.

Now that the hackers have gotten as much information as they can about your company, they know the vulnerabilities, they will then try to take advantage of those vulnerabilities through different methods, like:

- Sending out a phishing email  
- Conducting social engineering  
- Taking advantage of weak passwords  
- Unpatched software  
- Injecting malware  
- And so on  

---

## 3. Privilege Escalation

So at this point right now, the hackers, they've gained access.

What do you think they will do?

They're not just gonna start attacking.

No, there are things they will actually do first.

The first one here is to increase the control over the exploited target.

They do things like:

- Create new accounts  
- Hijack the network  

And then if they are able to gain access to the network via a non-admin account, they will then try as much as possible to actually take control of an admin account.

Because of course, admin accounts are a lot more powerful than regular accounts.

---

## 4. Establishing Persistence

Next is to Establish Persistence.

This means that they will try to ensure that they have continued access even after the brutal attack has been discovered by their targets.

And they can do things like:

- Create back doors  
- Establish remote access control  
- And so on  

---

## 5. Attack Phase

Once they've done this, next will now be to launch the actual attack phase.

- Data extraction  
- Data corruption  
- Malware injection  
- And more  

---

---

## Recap

So, to give you a quick recap:

1. **Reconnaissance Phase** – Gather information about the target  
2. **Exploitation Phase** – Take advantage of discovered weaknesses  
3. **Privilege Escalation** – Gain higher-level access  
4. **Establishing Persistence** – Maintain access over time  
5. **Attack Phase** – Execute the main attack  
6. **Cover Up** – Avoid detection  

These are the usual six steps that cyber criminals would perform in order to successfully attack a company.
