### **The Hacker Methodology: 6 Key Phases**

**1. Reconnaissance (Footprinting)**
The process of gathering as much information as possible about a target.
*   **Passive Reconnaissance:** Gathering publicly available information legally (e.g., social media, public records).
*   **Active Reconnaissance:** Probing systems directly for weaknesses, often illegal (e.g., scanning IP addresses or open ports).

**2. Exploitation**
Gaining initial access by taking advantage of discovered vulnerabilities.
*   **Methods:** Phishing, social engineering, weak passwords, unpatched software, or malware injection.

**3. Privilege Escalation**
Increasing the level of control over the system.
*   **Goal:** Gain administrative or "root" access to hijack networks or create new accounts.

**4. Establishing Persistence**
Ensuring long-term access to the target system even if the initial entry point is closed.
*   **Techniques:** Installing backdoors or remote access tools.

**5. Attack Phase**
Executing the actual objective of the hack.
*   **Actions:** Stealing data (extraction), corrupting information, or deploying malware.

**6. Covering Tracks**
Hiding evidence of the intrusion to avoid detection by security teams.
*   **Methods:** Clearing system logs, erasing command history, or using covert communication channels.

***

**Key Concept to Remember:** The success of an attack is heavily dependent on the amount of information the attacker gathers during the Reconnaissance phase.
