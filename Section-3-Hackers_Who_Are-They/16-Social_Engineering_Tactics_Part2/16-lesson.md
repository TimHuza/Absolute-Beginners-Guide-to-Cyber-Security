### **Workplace Social Engineering Tactics**

*   **Lunch Time Attack:** Occurs when employees leave their workstations without logging out. This allows "insider threats" to easily access sensitive information while the user is away.
    *   **Prevention:** Always lock or log out of your computer whenever you step away.
*   **Piggybacking:** An attacker gains entry to a restricted area by exploiting human kindness, such as someone holding a door open for them. This tactic relies on trust and social pressure.
*   **Tailgating:** Similar to piggybacking, but the attacker follows an authorized person into a restricted area without any direct interaction or permission. Attackers often blend in by acting confident.
*   **Shoulder Surfing:** The attacker observes a victim entering sensitive data, such as a password or PIN. This is a risk in public spaces like ATMs.
*   **Dumpster Diving:** Retrieving sensitive information from discarded materials like documents or old devices. This is common when people dispose of items improperly.
    *   **Prevention:** Properly shred all sensitive documents and physically destroy storage media.

### **Key Takeaways for Security**

*   **The Human Factor:** Technical security measures are ineffective if an attacker can exploit human behavior, trust, and routine actions. Even the strongest security systems can fail if people unknowingly allow access to an intruder.
*   **Best Practices for Prevention:**
    *   Train employees to recognize these tactics.
    *   Stay observant of unfamiliar individuals who lack proper access.
    *   Always verify identities and ask questions if something seems suspicious.
    *   Report any suspicious behavior to security immediately.
    *   Maintain awareness and cautious behavior at all times.
