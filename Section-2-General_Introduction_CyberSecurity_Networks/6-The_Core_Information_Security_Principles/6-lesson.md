# 6. The Core Information Security Principles

## 3 Key principles (CIA Triad)
There are 3 key principles that are the foundation of information security:

1. **Confidentiality**
2. **Integrity**
3. **Availability**

#### 1. Confidentiality
Means that data is only accessed by those who have the right to access the data

#### 2. Integrity
Integrity simply ensures that the data has not been tampered with, altered, and it's not been modified even if it was modified it is by the right people.

#### 3. Availability
Availability ensures that the data is available to those who have the right to access the data


## Supporting Principles
* **Authentication** - the process of verifying the identity
* **Authorization** - the process of approving access
* **Accounting** - the proccess of tracing actions to the source 

## Opposing Principles (DAD)
* **Disclosure**
* **Alteration**
* **Denialbility**

#### Disclosure
Disclosure means data is accessed by non-authorized users.

#### Alteration
Data has been compromised or tempered with malware, viruses, SQL injection.

#### Deniabilty
Data is not made available to those who need it

## Privacy
This refers to the right of an individual to control the use of their personal information.

## Non-repudiation
A subject cannot deny something such ass creating, modifying, or sending resource.
